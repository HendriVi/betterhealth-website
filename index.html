The BetterHealth method

The diagnosis matters, but by itself, it leaves too much unexplained.

A person comes in with symptoms of anxiety, depression, burnout, cognitive fatigue, panic, insomnia, loss of drive or the strange sense that they no longer fully recover from stress. We keep the diagnosis, because safety and medicine need a working structure. But then we go further. We ask which biological and psychological systems are keeping the person stuck.

01

From symptom to pattern

We map chronology, function, exposures, sleep, medication history, trauma, cognition and the exact way the symptom experienced has changed over time.

02

From pattern to system

We test our ranked hypotheses: metabolic strain, autonomic threat, sleep disruption, inflammatory load, endocrine transition, post-concussive change, or bioenergetic fragility.

03

From system to plan

Each intervention gets a target, a dose and direct measurement. Every change is done only when the data and the lived experience change, ever adapting to the situation.

Scientific structure

Mental health is produced inside a living system.

Our brain is predictive by its very nature; it’s even been called a prediction machine. It interprets the body, the world around and the expected future through its own predefined models of threat, safety, effort and relevance. When the body is sleep deprived, chronically inflamed, metabolically weak or energetically strained, our perception changes because of that. The world feels different because the system is reading and interpreting it differently.

Tap a card to open the explanation.

P

Perception and prediction

Following the logic of predictive processing and active inference, symptoms are understood as perception, body state and learned prediction all interacting in real time.

M

Mitochondrial energy

Mitochondria are our energy producers but also signaling hubs. They help determine whether a cell can adapt, repair, communicate and recover after demand. They play a crucial role in our overall health but also in our mental health, having been linked to anxiety, autism, ADHD, depression and even the onset of neurodegeneration.

A

Allostatic load

Stress is measured across exposure, appraisal, autonomic state, endocrine rhythm, inflammation, behavior and recovery. A single biomarker can show one signal. The whole story needs the pattern.
