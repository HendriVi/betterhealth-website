The BetterHealth method

The diagnosis matters. By itself, it leaves too much unexplained.

A person comes in with anxiety, depression, burnout, cognitive fatigue, panic, insomnia, loss of drive or the strange sense that their system no longer recovers. We keep the diagnosis, because safety and medicine need structure. Then we go further. We ask which biological and psychological systems are keeping the person stuck.

01

From symptom to pattern

We map chronology, function, exposures, sleep, medication history, trauma, cognition and the exact way the symptom behaves over time.

02

From pattern to system

We test ranked hypotheses: metabolic strain, autonomic threat, sleep disruption, inflammatory burden, endocrine transition, post-concussive change, or bioenergetic fragility.

03

From system to plan

Each intervention gets a target, dose, owner and measurement. The plan changes when the data and the lived experience fail to move together.

Scientific architecture

Mental health is produced inside a living system.

The brain is predictive. It interprets the body, the world and the future through models of threat, safety, effort and relevance. When the body is sleep deprived, inflamed, metabolically fragile or energetically strained, perception changes. The world feels different because the system is different.

P

Perception and prediction

Following the logic of predictive processing and active inference, symptoms are understood as perception, body state and learned prediction interacting in real time.

M

Mitochondrial energy

Mitochondria are energy producers, but also signaling hubs. They help determine whether a cell can adapt, repair, communicate and recover after demand.

A

Allostatic load

Stress is measured across exposure, appraisal, autonomic state, endocrine rhythm, inflammation, behavior and recovery. A single biomarker can show one signal. The whole story needs the pattern.

E

Evolutionary mismatch

Modern work, light, food, isolation, digital urgency and sleep compression can push ancient regulatory systems into patterns they were never built to carry chronically.
